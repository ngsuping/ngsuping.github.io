在马来西亚长大，高中毕业主动选择去上海交大读书——不是因为有人带路，是因为想去更大的地方看看，然后很努力地在那读了下来。本科信息安全，硕士管理科学，技术和商业两条线同时走，让我养成了一个习惯：遇到问题，先想有没有更系统的解法。

这个习惯在工作里体现得更明显。还没有 AI 的时候，我就在用 Excel 函数和脚本把重复的事自动化——不是因为懒，是因为觉得人的精力应该花在真正需要判断的地方。有了 Claude 之后，这件事变得更系统，SQL 生成、异动解读、报告初稿，整理成模板，沉淀成 SOP。

现在在美团做商业分析，也在认真探索怎么把 AI 真正嵌进分析工作流——不是追热点，是想搞清楚哪些东西值得自动化、哪些还是需要人来判断。

{{ else }}

I grew up in Malaysia and chose to study at Shanghai Jiao Tong University right after high school — no family roadmap, just curiosity about bigger places. Undergrad in Information Security, master's in Management Science: tech and business in parallel. That left me with one habit — when a problem shows up, ask first whether there's a more systematic way to solve it.

That habit shows up at work even louder. Long before AI, I was already automating repetitive tasks with Excel functions and scripts — not out of laziness, but because human attention should be spent on the parts that actually need judgment. With Claude, it became more systematic: SQL generation, anomaly interpretation, report drafting — patterned into templates, distilled into SOPs.

Now I'm doing business analysis at Meituan, and seriously exploring how to embed AI into the analytics workflow — not chasing trends, but figuring out which parts are worth automating and which still need a human in the loop.
